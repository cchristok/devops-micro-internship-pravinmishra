## Answer

I believe that progress is best measured quietly and over time , not loudly and immediately.Many people around me equate growth with speed how fast you learn a tool, how many hours you grind or how how quickly you announce results.My experience has shown the opposite sustainable progress comes from consistency, reflection, and systems that can be repeated even on difficult days.

## Truth #1 

### Truth
Small, conststent actions compound into big results

### Evidence from my life
When I committed to studying for just 45mins every day, even on the days I felt unmotivated, I was able to to understand topics better and complete more courses. In the past, I used to study in long bursts during weekends, which left me tired and forgetful. Daily consistency helped me stay on track and build real mastery over time.
---

## Truth #2

### Truth

Teaching or explaining something is the fastest way to truly understandy it.

### Evidence from my life

Whenever I write notes, create a tutorial, or explain a concept to a friend or in an online forum, I notice I unserstand the topic much deeper. For example, when I documented how I set up a VPN on Ubuntu, I found and fixed gaps in my own understanding. Sharing my knowledge not only helps others but strengthens my learning.

---

## Truth #3

### Truth

A healthy body and mind are non-negotiable for long term success.

### Evidence from my life

During periods when I sacrificed sleep, skipped workouts, and ate poorly, my focus dropped, and I made more mistakes. Once I prioritised 6-7 hours of sleep, regular exercise, and breaks, my energy improved, I stayed calmer under pressure, and I got more done with less stress.

# Reflection – Week 1

### Biggest insight I got about myself this week
I realized that I make the most progress when I focus on consistent daily practice rather than trying to learn everything at once. Small, focused efforts produce better long-term results than occasional intensive study sessions.

### My biggest weakness/loop I noticed

I sometimes spend too much time watching tutorials instead of applying what I've learned through hands-on labs and projects. I also tend to overthink before starting a task instead of taking action immediately.

### One system I will implement from this week (exact habit + time)
